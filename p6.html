Introduction

Theoretical physics is a branch of physics that employs mathematical models and abstractions to explain and predict natural phenomena. It is a fundamental part of the scientific method and plays a crucial role in advancing our understanding of the universe.

Key Concepts

Theoretical physics encompasses a wide range of concepts, including:

  • Quantum Mechanics
  • General Relativity
  • String Theory
  • Particle Physics
  • Cosmology

Applications

Theoretical physics has numerous applications in various fields, such as:

  • Developing new technologies
  • Understanding the fundamental forces of nature
  • Exploring the origins and fate of the universe
  • Advancing medical imaging techniques
